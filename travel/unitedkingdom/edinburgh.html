Edinburgh, Scotland

On our third weekend of the semester, we traveled to Edinburgh by train and toured the Old City and Holyrood Park.

We took a train from Lancaster Station to Edinburgh Waverley. It took around two hours and we got some nice views of the Scottish countryside.

High Street in Old Town, Edinburgh.

St. Giles' Cathedral, built in 1872. The church was founded in 1124.

A statue of Adam Smith (1723-1790), also known as "The Father of Economics".

Further West down High Street.

This former church, first opened in 1845 is now used as a public event center called "The Hub".

Edinburgh Castle. Built on Castle Rock, the oldest standing parts of the castle were built in the 1100s. There are cliffs on all sides of Castle Rock, except to the East.

Looking down from Castle Rock to the South.

battlement • /ˈbat(ə)lm(ə)nt/
a parapet at the top of a wall, especially of a fort or castle, that has regularly spaced squared openings for shooting through

They had a bunch of (non-functional) cannons set-up around the walls -- this is what a castle defender might have seen hundreds of years ago.

Since 1861, the One o'clock Gun has fired every day, minus Sundays, Good Friday, and Christmas. Guess when it fires!

The Scottish War Memorial. It has records of all Scottish servicemen and women dating back to 1914.

View from the top of the Castle. The train tracks on the bottom are where we arrived in Edinburgh.

I figured out that portrait mode on my phone actually takes some pretty cool shots.

The view to the South from the castle walls. Next, we walked all the way to Arthur's Seat, the hill on the far left of this photo.

If you look closely, you can see people on top of the peak in the distance.

This was the view from about half way up. We came up on the trail to the left.

Bekah, Cait, [Australian] Connor, [New York] Connor, Me, Gabby and Anežka.

Nice sunset as we were coming down and walking back to the main part of town.

The Palace of Holyroodhouse, the Queen's official residence in Scotland.

View of town from Calton Hill. This is where the Nelson Monument and National Monument of Scotland are located.

The Balmoral Hotel Clock Tower. This photo was taken at 5:02, but you may notice that the clock shows 5:05. Timekeepers run the clock 118 seconds to give passengers a bit of extra time to make it to their train.

The Scott Monument, named after Scottish writer Sir Walter Scott.

That's all for this trip!
