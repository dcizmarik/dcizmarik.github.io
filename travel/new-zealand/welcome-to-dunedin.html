Welcome to Dunedin

After three flights and nearly thirty hours of travel time, I arrived in Dunedin, NZ. The journey took me just shy of 15,000 kilometers (just over 9,000 miles) away from home.

Air New Zealand Flight 675, at Auckland International Airport.

The Water of Leith passes through campus and lets out into the Otago Harbour.

The First Church of Otago, built in 1868, located near The Octagon (town center).

The first weekend, a bunch of us decided to climb up Signal Hill (393m). We got an early start.

The start of the trail was next to Logan Park High School, a ten minute walk from our flat.

The track that we took up was a mountain bike path, so we occasionally had to dodge oncoming traffic. After a few minutes of hiking, I realized the hill was much steeper than it looks.

There was a flat area around half way up the hill. At this point I and a few others had started to separate from a group that was going much faster.

The hillsides are almost entirely covered by houses.

We decided to descend down the other side of the hill to look at Otago Harbour before going all the way to the top.

My first New Zealand train! We put some New Zealand coins on the tracks and waited for the train to pass over.

Views of Otago Harbor from the town of Ravensbourne.

Seth, Ben, Addie, Elise, Me, Mark and SaraJoy. It was really bright out.

View from the top of Signal Hill. The land all the way in the background is around 40 kilometers (25 miles) away.

I wore professional hiking gear for this one.

The path was lined with flowers. We were already coming down at 12pm.

After the first week of school, I took a trip by city bus to St. Clair Beach.

More houses on hills, this time overlooking the beach.

St. Clair beach eventually turns into St. Kilda beach if you walk south enough.

I was happy to get out of the flat and travel a bit farther from home.

Weather was a bit overcast, but the beach was still beautiful.
